332-63 BCE
Hellenistic Influence
After the Macedonian conquest of the Achaemenid Empire in 330 BC and its disintegration shortly thereafter in the Partition of Babylon and subsequent Wars of the Diadochi, Hellenistic kingdoms were established throughout West Asia), Northeast Africa, and South Asia.
636-1099 CE
Islamic Golden Age
Arab Muslims conquer Palestine. Jerusalem becomes the third holiest city of Islam. Beginning of the Arab-Islamic period.
1517-1917
Ottoman Empire
Palestine becomes part of the Ottoman Empire. The region develops under Ottoman administration.
1948
The Nakba
Establishment of the State of Israel. Over 700,000 Palestinians are expelled or flee. Beginning of the Palestinian diaspora.
3000 BCE
Peleset
The region of Palestine was already settled in the Bronze Age. Canaanites, Philistines, and other peoples lived in this fertile land between the Mediterranean and the Jordan.
63 BCE
Roman Empire
Palestine becomes part of the Roman Empire. The region is called "Palestine," derived from the Philistines.
1250-1516
Mamluk Sultanate
The Mamluk Sultanate, also known as Mamluk Egypt or the Mamluk Empire, was a state that ruled Egypt, the Levant and the Hejaz from the mid-13th to early 16th centuries, with Cairo as its capital. It was ruled by a military caste of mamluks (freed slave soldiers) headed by a sultan.
1967
Mandatory Palestine
Mandatory Palestine was a British administrative territory that existed between 1920 and 1948 in the region of Palestine, and after 1922, under the terms of the League of Nations' Mandate for Palestine. The British took the territory deeming it presently unfit for self-governance.
1920-1948
Six-Day War
Israel occupies the West Bank, Gaza Strip, East Jerusalem, and the Golan Heights. Beginning of the Israeli occupation.
